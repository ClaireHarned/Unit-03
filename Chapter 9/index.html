Mobility Patterns in the EU During the COVID-19 Pandemic: A Look at the Numbers of Moving People in 2020

The year 2020 was a unique year for migration in the European Union (EU) due to the COVID-19 pandemic. The pandemic's impact on international and domestic mobility was significant, resulting in several travel restrictions and mobility limitations. Consequently, the movement of people in EU countries in 2020 differed from the usual trends. There was a substantial decrease in international migration, while domestic migration increased in some EU countries. There were several disaster-related internal displacements in EU countries in 2020, as extreme weather events such as floods and storms forced many people to flee their homes. Additionally, the pandemic had a profound impact on refugees and asylum seekers, making their movements even more challenging. Understanding the numbers and patterns of moving people in EU countries in 2020 is crucial to gaining a comprehensive understanding of the pandemic's impact on migration and human mobility in the region. This map and graph demonstrates the patterns of these moving peoples throughout each EU country (excluding Sweden) and shows the differences of numbers within each category of moving people.
